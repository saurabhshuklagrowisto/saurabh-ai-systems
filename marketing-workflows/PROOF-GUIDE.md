# How to back each workflow with proof

The READMEs in this folder describe the architecture, the numbers, and the stack. Architecture is documentation. Numbers are claims. **Proof is what makes a hiring manager believe the claims.**

This guide explains what kind of proof works for each of the 6 marketing workflows, what you can add yourself, what you should ask for from past clients or your current employer, and what the hierarchy of credibility looks like.

## The four levels of proof, from weakest to strongest

1. **The number alone** ("25+ meetings/month") · believable, but unverifiable. Floor of credibility.
2. **The number plus the methodology** ("25+ meetings via the Clay + Apollo + Sendy + Claude flow described above") · stronger. Shows you know how the number was made.
3. **The number plus the artifact** ("25+ meetings — here is the Zoho CRM deals dashboard screenshot, the Sendy campaign summary, the n8n workflow JSON") · much stronger. The reader can see evidence.
4. **The number plus the artifact plus a third party** ("25+ meetings — here is the dashboard, plus a one-line testimonial from the AE who ran the calls") · strongest. Independent confirmation.

Top portfolios mix all four levels. Numbers alone signal nothing. Numbers plus the artifact and one third-party reference signal everything.

## Per-workflow proof checklist

### 01 · ABM Outbound to US eCommerce Founders

**You can add yourself:**
- A sanitised Lemlist sequence screenshot showing the multichannel cadence (email + LinkedIn steps)
- A Claude prompt example for the founder-level hook generation (the pattern in `claude-skills/abm-account-brief-skill/prompts/v2.md` serves)
- A redacted screenshot of a real founder outreach message (mask the recipient name + brand)

**Worth asking for:**
- Zoho CRM Deals dashboard screenshot showing the influenced pipeline number (with names blurred)
- Lemlist campaign summary screenshot showing the 6%+ reply rate
- Apollo screenshot showing the founder-level targeting depth
- A single line from the client or AE: "the ABM founder outbound booked us [X] meetings a month"

**Highest-impact artifact:** the Lemlist reply-rate screenshot. 6% on founder outbound is well above the 1 to 3% baseline; the screenshot is the credibility multiplier.

### 02 · ABM Playbook · 100 Named US Accounts

**You can add yourself:**
- A sanitised version of the buying-committee persona map for one example account (real account names removed)
- The ICP rubric used to pick the 100 accounts (you already have something like this in `production-systems/abm-automation/icp-criteria-example.json`)
- A diagram of the multi-channel cadence (LinkedIn paid + outbound + event)

**Worth asking for:**
- Zoho CRM ABM dashboard screenshot (account engagement scores, names blurred)
- LinkedIn Ads dashboard showing the retargeting performance
- A photo or screenshot from one of the 4 US conferences

**Highest-impact artifact:** the Zoho account-engagement dashboard. ABM is account-level, so the dashboard that shows account-level scores (not lead-level) is the proof that the program was actually run as ABM.

### 03 · Email and Newsletter Overhaul

**You can add yourself:**
- A before/after screenshot pair from Sendy showing the open-rate timeline
- A sanitised A/B testing framework doc (your own SOP)
- An example Claude prompt for first-draft email copy generation
- A sample subject-line variant set (5-10 variants Claude generated, no client name)

**Worth asking for:**
- A direct screenshot from Sendy or Looker Studio showing the 14% → 29% open-rate jump on the timeline
- The Looker Studio dashboard URL or screenshot showing the lifecycle metrics
- A client quote: "Our email engagement doubled after Saurabh took over the program"

**Highest-impact artifact:** the Sendy timeline screenshot showing the open-rate jump. A clear "before" and "after" with dates is the strongest possible proof — a hiring manager can see the inflection point.

### 04 · Webinar and Podcast Demand Engine

**You can add yourself:**
- The 4-week motion playbook (pre-event ABM, live scoring rules, post-event nurture branches)
- A sample branched nurture sequence (Hot / Engaged / No-show variants)
- The n8n workflow that does live engagement scoring during sessions

**Worth asking for:**
- Riverside dashboard screenshot showing audience engagement on one session
- Zoho Forms registration page conversion data
- A link to one of the public podcast episodes (if any are public-shareable)
- A client quote about the 10% conversion rate

**Highest-impact artifact:** a public podcast episode link. Lets the reader actually listen and judge for themselves that the program produced real content.

### 05 · Upwork CTO Account · End-to-End Motion

**You can add yourself:**
- A redacted PDF proposal generated by the Upwork plugin (mask the client name)
- A sanitised version of the cover letter format rules (already in your CHANGELOG)
- Screenshots of the `/upwork-scan` output (a ranked job list with names blurred)
- The 16-dimension scoring rubric structure (in the Upwork README)

**Worth asking for:**
- The Upwork profile URL (if public)
- A screenshot of the Upwork dashboard showing the account-level metrics (proposals sent, jobs won, earnings range)
- A client testimonial from an Upwork-acquired account
- A redacted earnings summary screenshot from Upwork

**Highest-impact artifact:** the Upwork profile URL. If the public profile shows job-success score and earnings range, the proof is right there.

### 06 · Marketing Ops Automation Layer

**You can add yourself:**
- A list of the 14 workflows with one-line descriptions of each
- A sanitised n8n workflow JSON export of one of the simpler workflows (e.g., the dedup workflow)
- The composite-tier scoring rubric (A/B/C) you wrote
- The weekly automation digest template (a sample email Claude generates)

**Worth asking for:**
- A screenshot of the n8n instance showing 14 active workflows (workflow names visible, but data fields blurred)
- The Looker Studio dashboard URL (could be a sanitised version)
- A client or team quote: "Saurabh's automation layer saves us ~12 hours a week"

**Highest-impact artifact:** the n8n workflows screenshot showing 14 live workflows. It is the most directly verifiable claim in the entire portfolio — the workflow count is right there in the screenshot.

## What you should NOT do

- **Do not fabricate screenshots.** If the artifact doesn't exist, leave the proof slot empty and rely on the architecture doc plus the number. Fabricated proof is worse than no proof.
- **Do not show real client names without permission.** Mask, blur, or anonymise. The permission audit pattern in `production-systems/upwork-proposals` exists for exactly this reason.
- **Do not pile every artifact into the README.** Pick the 1-2 strongest per workflow. Burying proof in clutter weakens it. Hosted external links (Drive, Loom) keep the README clean and the proof one click away.

## Suggested folder structure inside each workflow

```
marketing-workflows/<workflow-name>/
├── README.md                  what the workflow does, the numbers, the architecture
├── proof/
│   ├── dashboard-screenshot.png       (sanitised)
│   ├── n8n-workflow.json              (sanitised export, if applicable)
│   ├── prompt-examples.md             (sample Claude prompts, no client data)
│   └── testimonial.md                 (one-line quote, with permission)
└── case-study-link.md             link to the long-form narrative on shivsaurabh.netlify.app
```

Six workflows, each with a `proof/` subfolder. The subfolder is the credibility layer. Empty for now; add as artifacts become available.

## How a recruiter reads this

A senior recruiter spends 30 seconds on the repo README, 60 seconds skimming one workflow folder, and 10 seconds glancing at the proof subfolder. The proof subfolder is what tips them from "this looks good" to "I should reach out."

Build the architecture and numbers now (already done in the READMEs). Add proof artifacts as you collect them. **Even one strong artifact per workflow is enough to elevate the entire repo.**

## The 80/20

If you only have time to gather 3 artifacts across the workflows, prioritise:

1. **The Lemlist reply-rate screenshot** (ABM Founder Outbound) — the 6%+ number is the most impressive single metric in the portfolio
2. **The Sendy or Looker open-rate timeline screenshot** (Email Overhaul) — the 14% → 29% jump has a visible inflection point a hiring manager can see
3. **The n8n workflows screenshot showing 14 live** (Marketing Ops Automation) — the workflow count is directly verifiable

Those three, gathered and sanitised, raise the credibility of the entire portfolio by another full step.
